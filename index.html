In order to analyse which parts of the USA have high traffic border crossings, we have to analyse the following factors.

1. Identifying the highest traffic crossings: Trends show that Southern states of the USA bordering Mexico, mainly California and Texas have the highest traffic crossings. Firstly, this is due to the significantly higher population density on the USA-Mexico border. The top 3 ports, San Ysidro in California, El Paso, and Lardo in Texas are all direct ports to the biggest Mexican border cities like Tijuana, Ciudad Juarez, and Monterrey respectively. Hence, a greater density of cross-border movement for work, business, and family visits. Based on the table, we can see a positive correlation between population density and the number of border crossings.

2. Geospatial Distribution: Based on data from the table and the population density map, geospatial analysis reveals that states like Michigan, New York, and Washington and on the southern border with Mexico, California, Texas, Arizona, and New Mexico have the greatest number of border crossings. This distribution reflects the geographic proximity to major population centers, industrial hubs, and trade corridors.

3. Regional Development & Socioeconomic Impacts: Lastly, regional developments of the cities connected by the borders and socioeconomic factors like job opportunities and strong cultural influence separated by the borders affect the tendency for people to cross the border. As Mexico has a significantly lower GDP per capita compared to Canada, many more Mexicans are pushed to cross the border in order to access the more lucrative job market in the US.
